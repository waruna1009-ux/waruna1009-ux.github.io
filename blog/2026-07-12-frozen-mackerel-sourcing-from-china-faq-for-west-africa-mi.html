Frozen Mackerel from China: What Importers Need to Know

Short answer: China supplies Chub Mackerel (Scomber japonicus) and Horse Mackerel (Trachurus spp.) year-round from domestic catch + distant-water fleet. Forms range from Whole Round to Fillet to Butterfly, packed IQF or block frozen. Top buyer markets: West Africa, Middle East, Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe.

Key Species

  • **Chub Mackerel** — dominant export species, moderate oil content, firm flesh. Available 200–1000g+.
  • **Horse Mackerel** — slightly firmer, frequently supplied whole round or HGT.

Popular Product Forms by Market

| Market | Preferred Form | Common Size |

|---|---|---|

| West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana) | Whole Round, block frozen, ungutted | 300–500g / 400–600g |

| Middle East (Saudi, Egypt, UAE) | Whole Round gutted, Butterfly | 400–800g |

| Southeast Asia | Whole Round or HGT for processing | 200–400g |

| Eastern Europe | Butterfly, Fillet, Whole Round | 300–600g |

Quality Checklist

  • ✅ Clear eyes, bright red gills (pre-freeze freshness)
  • ✅ Bright silver skin, no freezer burn
  • ✅ Fresh sea odor — no rancid/off smell (oil content = oxidation risk)
  • ✅ Firm flesh when thawed, not mushy
  • ✅ Intact body — no head breakage or scale loss
  • ✅ Histamine control: -18°C cold chain from catch to container

Packing & Logistics

  • **IQF** — retail/foodservice, better portion control
  • **Block frozen** — bulk wholesale (dominant West African format)
  • **Transit:** W. Africa ~30–35 days, Middle East ~15–20 days, SEA ~5–10 days
  • **Certificates:** Health cert, COO, Halal (ME markets), EU Health (EU) — confirm per order
