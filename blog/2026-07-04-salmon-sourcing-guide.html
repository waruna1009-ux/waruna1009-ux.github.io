Frozen Salmon Sourcing from China: A Practical Guide for EU, US & Middle East Importers

**Disclaimer:** This guide provides general industry reference on frozen salmon product forms, processing, and sourcing from China. It does not constitute commercial advice, regulatory compliance advice, or a guarantee of any specific product, certification, or shipping outcome. Confirm all specifications, certificates, and shipping terms per order.

Why Source Frozen Salmon from China?

China is not a major salmon fishing or farming country — Atlantic salmon is primarily farmed in Norway, Chile, Scotland, the Faroe Islands, and Canada, while Pacific wild salmon comes mainly from Alaska and Russia. However, China has developed into a substantial salmon processing and value-added re-export hub, particularly in the port cities of Shandong (Qingdao, Yantai, Weihai) and Liaoning (Dalian).

The typical model: frozen whole Atlantic salmon (H&G or whole frozen) is imported from producing regions into China, where processing facilities perform scaling, heading, gutting, skinning, pin-bone removal, portioning, quick-freezing, and vacuum packaging. The resulting value-added products — salmon loins, portions, trims, bellies, skins — are then exported to end markets in Europe, North America, the Middle East, and Asia.

This model (sometimes called double-processing or re-export) gives buyers access to large-scale processing capacity, flexible product specifications, and consolidated order handling.

Product Forms: What Can Be Sourced

1. Frozen Whole Salmon / H&G (Headed & Gutted)

  • Whole frozen Atlantic salmon, head-on or head-off, gutted
  • Typically 4-7 kg per fish
  • Suitable for buyers with in-house processing capabilities or wholesale distribution

2. Frozen Salmon Loins & Portions

This is the most common import form for foodservice and retail:

  • **P-Loin (Portion-Sized Loin):** The premium cut from the backbone. Graded by weight — typical ranges: 4-6 oz, 6-8 oz, 8-10 oz, 10-12 oz. Ready-to-cook, consistent portion control. Most popular for restaurants, hotels, and meal kits.
  • **Tail-Loin:** Thinner cut from the tail section. Suitable for thin-slice applications, pan-searing, or saucing. Graded similarly by weight.
  • **Whole Fillet (Skin-On or Skinless):** Full fillet with or without skin, graded by weight range (e.g., 1-2 lb, 2-3 lb). Flexible for further processing.

Note: Portion sizes and grades are approximate industry references; confirm exact specifications per contract and batch.

3. Frozen Salmon Trims & Scrap

Processing by-products that offer excellent value for secondary applications:

  • **Belly Trim:** Fatty, flavorful cuts from the belly section. Sought after for Japanese cuisine (aburi, sashimi), Korean BBQ, and high-end grilling.
  • **Bloodline Trim:** Cuts from the bloodline area after the dark meat is removed. Suitable for canning, ready meals, and further processing.
  • **General Trim / Offcut:** Mixed cuts and pieces. Used in value-added products — surimi, fish cakes, fillings, pet treats, fish oil extraction.

4. Salmon Skin

  • **Skin-On Cuts:** Loin or fillet with skin attached for pan-searing (crispy skin applications).
  • **Skin as a By-Product:** Salmon skin has growing demand as a standalone ingredient — fried skin snacks, pet food, and collagen extraction.

5. Salmon Belly

  • High-fat content, rich mouthfeel, premium pricing segment.
  • Used in Japanese (aburi salmon belly), Korean (samgyeopsal-style), and Western fine-dining applications.

6. Salmon Frame / Bones

  • Frames, collars, and backbones after filleting.
  • Used for soup stock, broth, and fish oil. Popular in Asian foodservice channels.

7. Salmon Mince / Mince Block

  • Mechanically separated or hand-cut mince from processing offcuts.
  • Used in fish cakes, dumpling fillings, extruded products, and surimi blends.

Freezing & Packaging Methods

| Method | Description | Best For |

|--------|-------------|----------|

| IQF (Individual Quick Freeze) | Each portion frozen individually. Loose in box or vacuum-packed. | Retail, foodservice — portion control, flexible usage |

| Block Frozen | Multiple pieces frozen together in a block (10 kg or 20 kg). | Further processing, bulk distribution |

| Vacuum Pack (VP) | Individual or multiple portions vacuum-sealed. | Extended shelf life, freezer burn prevention, retail-ready |

| Modified Atmosphere (MAP) | Gas-flushed packaging. | Extended freshness, premium retail lines |

Packaging specifications (net weight per carton, piece count, bag type) are confirmed per buyer requirement and contract.

Quality Assessment Checklist

When evaluating frozen salmon, experienced buyers look at:

1. Color: Fresh/frozen Atlantic salmon should be orange-red to deep orange-red (from astaxanthin in feed). Even color distribution, no dark spots or bleaching. Pale color may indicate low astaxanthin or extended storage.

2. Fat Marbling: White fat striations (marbling) should be visible but not excessive. Well-marbled salmon provides better flavor and texture.

3. Glaze / Freezer Burn: Minimal ice glaze or clear glaze acceptable. No white/grey patches (freezer burn).

4. Thaw Yield (Key KPI): Thaw loss should typically be ≤ 5-8% depending on packaging and handling. Higher loss indicates quality degradation.

5. Smell: Fresh salmon scent — no sour, ammonia, or rancid notes upon thawing.

6. Packaging Integrity: No tears, leaks, or ice recrystallization (multiple freeze-thaw cycles).

Quality assessment is based on common trade practices. Actual inspection should be conducted per buyer specifications and destination market standards.

Sourcing Timing & Seasonality

  • **Year-round availability:** As a re-export product (relying on imported raw material + domestic processing capacity), frozen salmon products are generally available year-round.
  • **Q3 (July-September):** Supply can be slightly tighter due to European summer holidays and some seasonal raw material fluctuations.
  • **Q4 (October-December):** Pre-Christmas demand surge. Early ordering recommended.

Regulatory & Certification Considerations

  • **EU Market:** Frozen salmon imported into the EU requires an EU Health Certificate issued by the competent authority of the exporting country. Residue monitoring plans (RASFF notifications applicable). Check with your customs broker for current requirements.
  • **US Market:** FDA FCE (Food Canning Establishment) registration required for processing facilities exporting to the US. Prior Notice for each shipment. FDA Import Alert monitoring applies.
  • **Halal Certification:** Available upon buyer request — certification documents confirmed per product, partner plant, and order. Not assumed for all products.
  • **General:** Certification documents (health certificates, processing plant approvals, HACCP plans) are confirmed per product, partner plant, and destination market. We recommend verifying current requirements with a qualified customs broker or regulatory consultant before placing orders.

How to Start Sourcing

1. Define your product form — whole/H&G, loin portions, trims, belly, skin, or mince.

2. Confirm specifications — weight grading, packaging (IQF/block/VP), glaze percentage, skin-on/skinless.

3. Request certificates — EU Health Certificate, FDA registration, Halal — confirm per order.

4. Discuss shipping terms — FOB Qingdao/Dalian, CIF to your port, or DDP — per negotiation.

5. Arrange pre-loading inspection — can be arranged according to buyer requirements, before container loading.
