How to Source Frozen Salmon from China: A Buyer's Checklist

China processes imported Atlantic salmon into frozen portions at plants in Shandong and Liaoning. Buyers can source P-Loin, Tail-Loin, Whole Fillet, Trim, and Belly — typically IQF in vacuum packs. FOB Qingdao/Dalian, minimum 1x40ft container (~25 MT). Send product form, portion size, and destination to waruna825@outlook.com for a quotation.

Why Source Salmon Through China?

China is not a salmon farming country — but it is a major processing and re-export hub. The typical supply chain: raw frozen Atlantic salmon (H&G or whole) is imported from Norway, Chile, or the Faroe Islands. Processing facilities in Shandong (Qingdao, Yantai) and Liaoning (Dalian) handle scaling, skinning, portioning, pin-bone removal, and IQF freezing. The finished frozen portions are then exported to end markets.

This model gives buyers access to large-scale processing capacity, flexible portion specifications, and consolidated export handling from a single supplier.

Product Forms: What You Can Order

P-Loin (Portion-Sized Loin)

The premium cut from the backbone. Most common for restaurants, hotels, and meal kit providers. Standard weight ranges:

  • 4-6 oz (113-170g)
  • 6-8 oz (170-227g)
  • 8-10 oz (227-283g)
  • 10-12 oz (283-340g)

Tail-Loin

Thinner cut from the tail section. Suitable for pan-searing, thin-slice applications, or value menus. Graded by similar weight ranges.

Whole Fillet (Skin-On or Skinless)

Full fillet, graded by weight (e.g., 1-2 lb, 2-3 lb). Flexible for buyers with in-house further processing.

Trim, Belly, and Other Cuts

Salmon trim and belly are available for smoking, further processing, or pet food grade applications. Confirm grade and fat content per order.

Freezing and Packing

  • IQF (Individually Quick Frozen) — Most common for portions. Each piece frozen separately, easy to portion out.
  • Block Frozen — Products frozen together in blocks. Lower cost, suitable for further processing.
  • Vacuum Packed (VP) — IQF portions individually vacuum-sealed before case packing. Best shelf life.
  • MAP (Modified Atmosphere Packaging) — For retail-ready products. Confirm plant capability.

Key Questions to Ask Your Supplier

  1. What is the fat content range? Affects yield and cooking performance. Specify your target (e.g., 8-12% or 12-16%).
  2. What is the thaw yield? Typically ≤5-8% loss for well-processed frozen salmon. Confirm per batch.
  3. Bones removed? Pin-bone removal is standard for P-Loin and fillets. Confirm process.
  4. Added water/glaze? Protective ice glaze is standard. Net weight vs. gross weight must be clear in the contract.
  5. Country of origin of raw material? Affects tariff treatment. Norwegian, Chilean, and Faroese raw material may have different duty rates.
  6. Documentation? Health certificate, certificate of origin, catch/processing certificate — confirm per destination.

Shipping and Logistics

Main export ports: Qingdao and Dalian. Approximate ocean transit times:

  • EU (Rotterdam/Hamburg): 25-35 days
  • US West Coast (Los Angeles): 12-18 days
  • US East Coast (New York): 20-30 days
  • Middle East (Dubai/Jebel Ali): 18-25 days
  • Southeast Asia: 7-14 days

Times vary by carrier, season, and port congestion. Reefers at -18°C or below throughout transit.

Typical FOB Pricing Factors

Frozen salmon FOB pricing depends on:

  • Product form (P-Loin premium vs. Trim economy)
  • Portion size (8-10 oz typically commands higher price than 4-6 oz)
  • Raw material origin (Norwegian vs. Chilean)
  • Order volume and contract terms
  • Current raw material market price (follows Nasdaq Salmon Index)
