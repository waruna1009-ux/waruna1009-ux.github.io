Frozen Mackerel Sourcing Guide: 5 Key Spec Fields Importers Need to Know

If you are importing frozen mackerel from China — whether for West Africa, Southeast Asia, or the Middle East — your inquiry should cover species, product form, size grading, packing method, and destination documentation. This guide explains each field and why it matters.

Product reference: Frozen Mackerel →

1. Species Confirmation

Three mackerel species are commonly traded from China. Each has different end-use preferences by market:

  • Pacific mackerel (Scomber japonicus) — The dominant China export species. Wild-caught by purse seine in the Northwest Pacific (FAO 61), landed in Shandong. Primary markets include West Africa (whole round for retail/smoking), Southeast Asia (canning raw material), and Middle East (gilled & gutted for HORECA).
  • Chub mackerel — Often grouped with Pacific mackerel in trade. Used interchangeably in canning and food service.
  • Horse mackerel (Trachurus spp.) — A separate genus. Preferred in some West African markets (Nigeria, Ghana) for smoking and processing.

State the target species in your inquiry. If flexible on species, note which alternatives are acceptable.

2. Product Form

FormBest For
Whole RoundWest African retail, smoking, further processing
Gilled & Gutted (GG)Middle East / North Africa HORECA — higher cleanliness requirement
ButterflyReady-to-cook, food service
Fillet (skin-on/boneless)Canning, institutional kitchens

Whole round is the most traded form for Africa-bound shipments. GG commands a premium where kitchen prep time is limited.

3. Size Grading

Common grades from China processing plants:

  • 100-150g / 150-200g — Smaller sizes for canning or lower-tier retail
  • 200-300g — Entry-level whole round, popular in West African markets
  • 300-500g — The most commonly requested grade; balances yield with per-piece value
  • 500g+ — Premium grade for higher-end channels

Your optimal size depends on end-buyer price sensitivity and product format. A 300-500g whole round is a safe starting point for most wholesale inquiries.

4. Packing and Freezing Method

IQF (Individually Quick Frozen) — Fish frozen individually. Easier to portion. Standard for retail-ready repacking.

BQF (Block Quick Frozen) — Fish frozen in a block (typically 20kg). More economical. Common for wholesalers who reprocess.

Standard packing: 20kg carton with inner PE bag. MOQ typically 1×40' FCL (~25MT). Vacuum packing or interleaved sheets can be specified for premium orders.

5. Destination Documentation

Discuss with your supplier which documents are available as standard:

  • Health Certificate — Issued by CIQ (China Inspection and Quarantine)
  • Certificate of Origin — Form A or CO depending on destination
  • Fumigation Certificate — Required if wooden packaging is used
  • RFMO Catch Documentation — May be requested for certain markets and species

FAQ

Q: Which mackerel species is best for canning?
A: Pacific mackerel (Scomber japonicus) is the most common choice. Its firm flesh holds up to retort processing.

Q: What size should I inquire for West African markets?
A: 300-500g whole round is the most requested. Some markets also accept 200-300g.

Q: Is IQF worth the extra cost for bulk shipments?
A: IQF adds repacking flexibility. If selling to multiple smaller buyers, IQF allows portioning without thawing a full block.

Q: What documents are needed for mackerel import to the Middle East?
A: Typically Health Certificate, COO, and commercial invoice. Some UAE/Saudi importers request Halal processing documentation.
