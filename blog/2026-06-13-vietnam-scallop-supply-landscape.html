Vietnam Scallop Supply Landscape 2026: What Frozen Scallop Buyers Should Check

2026-06-13 · 采购指南

For frozen scallop meat buyers, Vietnam has become an increasingly visible processing and trading node in 2025–2026 market discussions. The practical sourcing question is not only origin — buyers should confirm size, moisture/glaze, net weight, packing, documents and destination-market requirements before requesting a firm quote.

Many importers are watching Vietnam because scallop processing and re-export activity has become more visible in 2025-2026 market discussions. According to VASEP data cited by Baird Maritime (April 2026) and Undercurrent News (March 2026), Vietnam's scallop exports reached approximately $66 million in 2025 (+49% year-on-year), with early 2026 momentum showing $18.1 million in January-February combined (+166% YoY). For B2B buyers, this creates a useful sourcing angle: compare available product forms, quality grades and document readiness before committing to one supplier route.

What should buyers verify before switching scallop supply?

Buyers should start with product identity and specification. For Japanese sea scallop meat, the common inquiry fields include size such as U15, U16-20, U21-25 and U26-30, moisture or glaze level, net weight, packing, carton marks, destination port and required certificates. A supplier should be able to confirm these points per order, not simply send a generic product name.

Key specification checklist for scallop meat inquiries

  • Size grade: U15, U16-20, U21-25, U26-30 (pieces per kg)
  • Moisture / glaze: Confirm percentage, net weight vs gross weight
  • Packing: 1kg/bag × 10kg/carton is common; confirm buyer preference
  • Quantity: Full container load (FCL) or less (LCL)
  • Destination port: Trade term (FOB, CNF, CIF) affects cost breakdown
  • Documents: Health certificate, certificate of origin, plant certificates and any destination-specific requirements

How should 2026 market data be used?

Market data such as the VASEP figures above (sources: Baird Maritime, 2026-04-10; Undercurrent News, 2026-03-25, both citing VASEP, covering Vietnam scallop export/processing sector) provides directional context for buyers evaluating supply options. It should be treated as a market signal, not a guarantee of continued trends. Buyers should verify current pricing and availability per their specific requirements.

Buyer FAQ

Can you quote scallop meat immediately?

A useful quote needs size, moisture or glaze, packing, quantity, destination port and required documents. Without those details, any price is only a rough discussion point.

Can certificates be guaranteed?

No certificate should be promised generically. Documents are checked per product, partner plant and order, and should match the buyer's destination-market requirements.

What is the best next step for importers?
