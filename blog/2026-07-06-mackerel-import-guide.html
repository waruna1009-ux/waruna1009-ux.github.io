China Mackerel: Species and Fishing Grounds

The primary species exported from China is Scomber japonicus (chub mackerel / Pacific mackerel / 鲐鱼). Fishing grounds include the Yellow Sea, East China Sea, and North Pacific. China is one of the world's largest producers and exporters of frozen mackerel, supplying markets across Africa, Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Europe.

Land-Frozen vs. Sea-Frozen

This is a critical specification that affects quality and price:

  • Land-Frozen (陆冻): Fish caught, transported to shore, processed and frozen at onshore plants. Typically better quality control and grading consistency. Slightly higher cost.
  • Sea-Frozen (船冻): Fish caught and immediately frozen on board the fishing vessel. Shorter time from catch to freeze — potentially better freshness. Grading may be less precise.

Both methods are widely used. The choice depends on your quality requirements and market. Specify your preference when requesting a quotation.

Packing Options

  • 10kg carton: Standard for most export markets. Easier handling.
  • 20kg carton: Lower packaging cost per kg. Suitable for large-volume buyers.
  • IQF option: Individually quick frozen fish (not frozen together as a block). Premium, easier to portion.
  • Block frozen: Fish frozen together in the carton. Standard, lower cost.

FOB Pricing Factors

Frozen mackerel FOB pricing is influenced by:

  1. Size grade: 300-500g typically 15-30% premium over 100-200g
  2. Freezing method: Land-frozen may carry small premium for quality control
  3. Seasonality: Fat content varies seasonally (higher fat in autumn/winter — preferred for grilling markets like Japan/Korea). Prices fluctuate with fishing seasons.
  4. Catch volumes: Subject to annual fishing quotas and natural stock variations
  5. Order volume: Standard minimum 1x40ft container (~25-27 MT)
  6. Destination requirements: Additional testing or certification adds cost

Import Documentation

Standard documents for frozen mackerel import:

  • Health Certificate (issued by China Customs/CIQ)
  • Certificate of Origin (Form E for ASEAN, Form A for EU GSP, etc.)
  • Catch Certificate (required for EU imports under IUU regulations)
  • Bill of Lading
  • Packing List
  • Commercial Invoice

Additional requirements may apply per destination country — confirm before contracting.

Key Questions to Ask Your Mackerel Supplier

  1. What size grade is being quoted? Confirm weight range and tolerance.
  2. Land-frozen or sea-frozen? Which plant?
  3. What is the catch date / production date? (Affects remaining shelf life)
  4. Is the price FOB or CFR? Which port?
  5. What glaze/water content? (Standard for whole round mackerel is minimal, but confirm)
  6. Can you provide catch certificate for EU import?
  7. What is the packing — 10kg or 20kg carton? IQF or block?
