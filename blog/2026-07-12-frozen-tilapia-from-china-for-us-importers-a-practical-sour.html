Frozen Tilapia from China for US Importers: A Practical Sourcing Guide on Fillet Grades, Shrimp-Style Packing & Quality Checklist

**Disclaimer**: This article is based on general industry knowledge. Specifications, quality benchmarks, and logistics timelines are approximate and may vary by supplier, batch, and market. Always verify current requirements with your supplier and authorities.

Why US Importers Look to China for Frozen Tilapia

China is one of the world's largest tilapia producers and exporters. For US importers, Chinese frozen tilapia offers year-round supply, diverse processing options, and established cold-chain logistics. The most common species is Nile Tilapia (Oreochromis niloticus), farmed primarily in Hainan, Guangdong, Guangxi, and Yunnan — with processing and export concentrated in coastal hubs like Fujian (Fuqing, Xiamen), Shandong (Qingdao, Rizhao), and Guangdong (Zhanjiang).

Key Processing Forms

| Form | US Market Use |

|------|---------------|

| Skin-on Fillet | Most common. Retail, food service |

| Skinless Fillet | Premium. Higher processing cost |

| Portion-cut | Uniform weight segments. Chain restaurants |

| Loin | Thickest back muscle. Highest value cut |

| Whole Round | Ethnic markets, wholesale |

| HGT (Headed, Gutted) | Further processing |

Premium fillets have the dark muscle (red meat) along the back thoroughly removed.

Size Grades (US Standard)

Fillet (oz per piece): 3-5 oz (value), 5-7 oz (most common), 7-9 oz (premium), 9-11 oz+ (upscale).

Whole Round (g per fish): 300-500 g, 500-800 g (most common), 800-1000 g+, 1000 g+.

Shrimp-Style Packing — What Is It?

A very common format for the US market: IQF fillets are layered in a specific pattern inside a tray or mold before freezing (e.g., 2 rows × 3 layers). The result is a neat, uniform block that is space-efficient for container loading and makes count verification easy. Other packaging options include IQF vacuum bags and block frozen (BQF).

Quality Checklist for US Importers

Color: Thawed fillet should be white to off-white. Yellowish or pinkish = storage issue.

Thawing Yield: Premium ≥ 95%. Standard 90-95%. Below 90% = concern.

Glaze: Typically 5-15%. Confirm contract net weight includes or excludes glaze.

Texture: Firm, springy, intact. Mushy or separating = reject.

Inspection: Factory pre-loading or third-party (SGS, Intertek, BV) — can be arranged per buyer requirements.

Logistics & Transit (Approximate)

| To | Transit |

|----|---------|

| US West Coast (LA/Long Beach) | 12-18 days |

| US East Coast (NY/Savannah) | 20-30 days |

Container: 20' reefer ~20-25 MT, 40' reefer ~26-28 MT. LCL for trial orders.

Seasonality

Q3-Q4 is peak harvest season (new crop starts arriving from July). Q1-Q2 has stable supply from mature stock. The typical grow-out cycle is about 4-6 months.

Quick Tips for First-Time Buyers

1. Define your spec (grade, size, packaging, glaze %)

2. Request a detailed spec sheet from suppliers

3. Order frozen samples via cold-chain air freight

4. Start with LCL or 1×20' container as trial

5. Verify import requirements with your customs broker
