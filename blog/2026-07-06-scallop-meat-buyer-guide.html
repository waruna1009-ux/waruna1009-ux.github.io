Japanese Scallop Meat from China: Sizes, Packing & What to Ask Before Ordering

Frozen Japanese sea scallop meat from China is available in U15, U16-20, U21-25, and U26-30 size grades. Standard packing is 1kg vacuum bag, 10 bags/carton. What Is Japanese Sea Scallop Meat?

Japanese sea scallop meat (Patinopecten yessoensis) is the adductor muscle of the scallop — the cylindrical white meat portion. It is also known as in Chinese. The product is distinct from bay scallops or other smaller scallop species. Japanese scallop meat is valued for its sweet flavor, firm texture, and versatility in sushi, hot pot, grilling, and Western cuisine.

China processes and re-exports Japanese scallop meat, with raw material sourced from Japanese and other fishing grounds, processed at plants primarily in Shandong and Liaoning provinces.

Size Grades: U-Scale Explained

The "U" designation means "under" — U15 means under 15 pieces per kilogram. Smaller numbers = larger pieces = higher price.

Packing Standards

  • Primary packaging: 1kg vacuum-sealed plastic bag (standard for all sizes)
  • Secondary packaging: 10 bags per master carton (10kg net per carton)
  • Palletization: Typically ~100 cartons per pallet (~1,000kg), but varies by carton dimensions
  • Container load: ~25 MT per 40ft reefer (approximately 2,500 cartons)

Glaze and Water Content — The Most Important Question

This is the #1 factor that affects your actual cost. Frozen scallop meat is typically protected with an ice glaze. The key distinction:

  • Net weight = scallop meat only (after glaze removed)
  • Gross weight = scallop meat + ice glaze

Standard industry glaze is 10-20%. This must be explicitly stated in your contract. A price quoted on "gross weight with 20% glaze" is actually 25% more expensive per kg of actual meat than the same price on "net weight."

Always confirm: "Is the price based on net weight or gross weight? What is the glaze percentage?"

What to Check Before Ordering

  1. Size grade uniformity: Ask for size distribution tolerance. A U16-20 grade should have the majority of pieces in that range.
  2. Color and appearance: Should be creamy white to light beige. Discoloration may indicate age or poor handling.
  3. Broken pieces: Specify maximum acceptable broken piece percentage (typically ≤5%).
  4. Glaze percentage: Confirm in writing. This directly impacts your real cost per kg of usable meat.
  5. Freezing method: IQF is standard for premium grades. Block frozen is available at lower cost for processing buyers.
  6. Shelf life: Typically 24 months at -18°C or below. Confirm production date before shipment.
  7. Documentation: Health certificate, certificate of origin, catch certificate, and any destination-specific requirements.

Typical FOB Pricing Factors

Scallop meat pricing depends primarily on:

  • Size grade (U15 commands highest premium)
  • Net vs. gross weight basis
  • Raw material availability (seasonal, varies by fishing quotas)
  • Order volume
  • Destination documentation requirements
