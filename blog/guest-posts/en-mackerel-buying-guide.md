If you're importing frozen mackerel in bulk, you already know the market has shifted. Norwegian mackerel — long the gold standard for quality — reached record export prices in January 2026. For buyers serving price-sensitive markets (West Africa, Southeast Asia, parts of the Middle East), the search for alternatives has accelerated.

China's Pacific mackerel has become the answer. But sourcing it correctly takes more than just finding the lowest FOB quote.

## Know your species and specs

China exports two main types: Pacific mackerel (Scomber japonicus, HS 0303.54) and a smaller volume of Atlantic mackerel (Scomber scombrus). The Pacific variety dominates — wild-caught by purse seine in the North Pacific, landed and processed in Shandong province.

Standard sizing:
- 200/300g per piece — entry-level, popular in West African markets
- 300/500g — the workhorse grade, used for canning, grilling, and food service
- 500g+ — premium grade, often exported to Japan and Korea

Always specify whether you want IQF (Individually Quick Frozen) or BQF (Block Quick Frozen). IQF costs slightly more but gives buyers flexibility in portioning. BQF is cheaper to ship but requires thawing the entire block.

## The real FOB price range (May 2026)

For 300/500g whole round IQF Pacific mackerel, current FOB Qingdao/Yantai prices range from **$800 to $1,200/MT**, with the spread determined by:
- Order volume (1×40' FCL ≈ 25 MT is the standard MOQ)
- Freezing method (IQF premium over BQF: roughly $50-80/MT)
- Certification level (BRC Grade A plants command a $30-50/MT premium)
- Seasonal timing (October-December peak season can push prices up 5-10%)

If someone quotes you $500/MT for 300/500g, walk away. It's either undersized, poorly graded, or coming through too many middlemen.

For reference: Norwegian mackerel was trading well above $2,000/MT in early 2026 — more than double the Chinese price for comparable sizing.

## Three questions to ask every supplier

Before you sign a contract, ask these:

1. **"Which port does your fish land at?"** — The answer should be specific (Shidao, Yantai, Qingdao, Zhoushan). Vague answers like "Shandong" suggest the supplier doesn't have direct factory relationships.

2. **"Send me your last three SGS inspection reports."** — If they only have in-house lab reports, that's a red flag. Third-party testing (SGS, Eurofins, Intertek) is standard for serious exporters.

3. **"What's your procedure if the container arrives with quality issues?"** — A professional exporter will have a clear protocol: photos within 48 hours, joint survey, compensation options (replacement shipment, price reduction, next-order credit). Anyone who says "that never happens" is either inexperienced or dishonest.

## Shipping and logistics

From Qingdao/Yantai to key destinations:
- West Africa (Lagos, Tema, Abidjan): 35-45 days
- Southeast Asia (Manila, Bangkok, Jakarta): 15-25 days
- Middle East (Jebel Ali, Dammam): 20-25 days
- East Africa (Mombasa): 25-30 days

Always confirm whether the quote includes documentation fees, THC, and fumigation. Some suppliers quote a low FOB price and then add $150-200 in document charges.
